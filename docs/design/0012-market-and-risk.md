## Context

The operator asked for deep introspection on the **market API** and **risk management**: trading must be efficient and must not be gameable by other players. The market is the one subsystem where the adversary is *every other player simultaneously*, the interface is anonymous, and a single bad pricing rule converts directly into credit transfer to a rival. The review confirmed the current trust guard is statistically weak (IBEX-018, confirmed): a count/volume/stddev gate over **only the latest history day**, with the code's own TODOs admitting the sanity check is unimplemented.

### What Ibex trades today, and with what guards (assessment)

**Flow.** `TerminalMission` tiers each known resource in each terminal room into reserve / transfer-out / passive-sale / active-sale bands (`missions/terminal.rs:100-126`) over `get_desired_storage_amount` (200k energy, flat 10k everything else, `missions/constants.rs:3-8`), and files requests into the `OrderQueue` (`terminal.rs:276-301`). `OrderQueueSystem` consumes them every 20 ticks under a `CpuBar::HighPriority` bucket gate when the feature flags allow (`transfer/ordersystem.rs:330-333`).

- **Sells (anything).** Every resource present in storage/terminal plus a base list (`terminal.rs:128-152`) is sellable once it exceeds its thresholds: **passive** sell orders in fixed 2,000-unit blocks priced at `avg_price + 0.1·stddev` of the latest history day (`ordersystem.rs:148-157, :372-381`), and **active** deals into existing buy orders requiring `remaining > 2000` and net price ≥ `avg − 0.2·stddev` after subtracting per-unit energy cost (`ordersystem.rs:248, :256-260, :415-420`).
- **Buys (passive only, default OFF).** Passive buy orders for energy and the 7 base minerals behind `market.buy_energy`/`buy_minerals` flags — **all market flags default `false` and `credit_reserve` defaults 10M** (`features.rs:135-150`), checked at `ordersystem.rs:330`. When a resource is below half its reserve target, a quarter of the deficit is requested (`terminal.rs:229-239`) and a buy order is placed at... `avg_price + 0.1·stddev` (`ordersystem.rs:449-460`) — the **same +0.1σ premium formula as the sell side**, i.e. Ibex offers to buy *above* the daily average unconditionally.
- **Intra-empire sends.** `run_mission` picks the best cross-room terminal delivery ranked by `value = resources/cost` with `TransferCapacity::Infinite` and no floor on the ratio (`terminal.rs:489-523`; `transfer/transfersystem.rs:2084-2107`; fractional cost at `transfer/utility.rs:4-8`) — a far room can win with an arbitrarily bad ratio if it is the only candidate.

**Guards that exist:** `can_trust_history = transactions > 100 && volume > 1000 && stddev ≤ 0.5·avg`, inspecting **only `.last()`** (`ordersystem.rs:349-351`); the credit-reserve floor on buying (`:330`); the 20-tick cadence + bucket bar (`:333`); auto-cancel of fully-filled orders (`:340-344`); the active-sell path *does* net out energy cost per unit (`:256-260`) — the review notes this, so far-room honeypots are already partially defused.

**Defects beyond IBEX-018's text, found in this pass (folded into the design, not re-flagged):**
1. **Energy-cap/deal-amount mismatch:** the active-sell path computes `transferable_units` (energy-bounded) for the minimum-size check, but then **deals the un-capped `transfer_amount`** (`ordersystem.rs:262-270` vs `:282`). The engine API rejects the whole deal when terminal energy < transfer cost (`engine\game\market.js:131-132`), so this wastes the deal opportunity rather than losing resources — a silent efficiency leak.
2. **Buy premium inversion:** maker buys at `avg + 0.1σ` (above) — a maker should bid *below* the reference value; the current formula donates the spread.
3. **No order lifecycle at all:** an order with `remaining_amount > 0` is never repriced, never expired deliberately, never de-backed — it sits at its creation price until filled (adverse selection: after any market move, our stale quote is free money for whoever notices first). The TODOs at `ordersystem.rs:127, :178` ("Handle order in progress, cancel etc.?") and `:367/:446` ("Validate that the current average price is sane... Need better pricing calculations") admit this.
4. **No exposure accounting:** nothing tracks credits spent or volume traded per day; the only bound is the 2k block size and the credit-reserve floor.

**Net:** today's system is safe mostly by **abstinence** (flags default off) and **block size** (2k caps per-order damage). The moment ADR 0010 L3 flips `buy_minerals` on, the weak trust model becomes a funded attack surface.

### Engine ground truth (cited; spot-verified in `engine\processor\global-intents\market.js` and `engine\game\market.js`)

- **Fees.** `createOrder` charges `ceil(price × totalAmount × 0.05)` **immediately** (`market.js:127-133`). `changeOrderPrice` **upward** charges `ceil(Δprice × remainingAmount × 0.05)`; **downward is free** (`:183-211` — the fee branch only fires for `newPrice > order.price`). `extendOrder` charges 5% on the added amount (`:225`). **Cancel forfeits the remaining fee** — the order is removed with no credit (`:502-505`). **Natural expiry after 30 real-time days refunds the unfilled portion's fee** (`:507-533`; `MARKET_ORDER_LIFE_TIME`, `common\constants.js:375`). Cap 300 orders/player (`game\market.js:90-93`).
- **Orders are continuously truth-checked.** Every tick the displayed `amount` is recomputed from actual backing: sell orders from terminal stock (`market.js:542-567`), buy orders from the owner's credits and terminal free space (`:569-587`). Unbacked orders show amount 0 / inactive. **But buy orders escrow nothing** — credits are checked only at execution (`:330-336`): a "buy wall" can be hollowed by spending the credits elsewhere the tick it would fill.
- **deal() resolution.** All players' deals are collected and **sorted by world-wrapping room distance, closest dealer first** (`market.js:289`). Fills cascade-clip to remaining amount, seller stock, buyer space, buyer credits (`:315-336`). **The dealer's terminal pays the energy cost and takes the 10-tick cooldown regardless of direction** (`:339` passes the dealer's terminal as the fee terminal; `executeTransfer` charges it, `:34-47`; cooldown at `:391-396`) — so one deal per terminal per 10 ticks, and **maker orders externalize both transfer energy and cooldown to the counterparty**. A failed/zero-clipped deal sets no cooldown (`:391-396` is inside the success branch). Max 10 `deal` intents/player/tick (`game\market.js:149-151`); orders repriced this tick are skipped for dealing (`_skip`, `:179-181`) — no same-tick reprice sniping in either direction. The API pre-validates energy, stock, and credits against the tick-start snapshot and returns errors rather than half-executing (`game\market.js:108-152`).
- **Transaction cost.** `ceil(amount × (1 − e^(−d/30)))`, d = wrapped room distance (`utils.js:644-659`) — cost/unit rises to 28% at d=10, 49% at d=20, 63% at d=30. Ibex's `calc_transaction_cost_fractional` mirrors this correctly (`transfer/utility.rs:4-8`).
- **History is poisonable.** There is **no same-user check anywhere in the terminal-deal path** (`market.js:291-398` never compares `order.user` to the dealer) — self-fills are legal trades and enter the backend's per-day history (`getHistory` returns up to 14 daily records: avg, stddev, volume, transaction count). The history a price model reads is whatever anyone was willing to pay 5% + transfer energy to print.

### Hard constraints

Tick-snapshot concurrency: we can never react to a same-tick fill or reprice; everything is **pre-committed pricing** — which is exactly why the price model, not reaction speed, is the whole defense. Single-threaded WASM; CPU = execution + intents (the expensive part is `get_all_orders` deserialization, not the ~0.2-CPU intents); VM-reset resilience (risk counters must be derivable or persisted per [0002](0002-serialization.md)); incremental migration with labelled breaks.

### Threat model — what an attack actually costs the attacker

A defense is only justified where the attack is cheap. Costing each attack against the fee/cost formulas above:

| # | Attack | Engine mechanics | Attacker's cost | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T1 | **Wash-trade history painting** — self-fill own order from own nearby terminal at a fake price to set the day's avg/stddev/volume | No same-user check (`market.js:291-398`); fills enter history; filled fee is never refunded | 5% of *painted notional* + transfer energy (~3.3% of volume at d=1) + dealer cooldowns (100 prints ≈ 1,000 ticks on one terminal). Worked example: paint 1,000 units of a P\*-priced mineral at 10·P\* → sunk ≈ 500·P\*; if Ibex then buys 2,000 units at ~10·P\*, attacker nets ≈ 18,000·P\*. **The current gate is satisfiable by construction** — a uniform-price wash day has stddev ≈ 0, easily passing `stddev ≤ 0.5·avg`, and `transactions>100, volume>1000` are trivial | **CHEAP, ~36× ROI → primary defense target** |
| T2 | **Spike-and-dump on a thin book** — eat real asks (taker, no fee) to print a high-avg day, then sell into the victim's avg-anchored buy | Taker pays no fee, only spread + transfer energy; history aggregates real fills | Crossing the spread on an illiquid resource — small on T3/commodities, prohibitive on energy | **CHEAP on illiquid resources → defend (liquidity floor + anchors)** |
| T3 | **Hollow buy wall** — post deep bids, spend the credits elsewhere when they would fill | No escrow; credits checked at execution (`:330-336`); fee **refunded at expiry if unfilled** (`:507-533`) | ≈ 0 long-run (fee round-trips through expiry; only optics + an order slot). Victim's deal zero-clips harmlessly (no cooldown on failure) but the victim *planned* against phantom liquidity | **FREE → defend: never treat resting depth as realizable liquidity** |
| T4 | **Backed sell-wall spoofing** — fake supply pressure with real stock | Sell display requires real terminal stock every tick (`:542-567`); cancel forfeits fee; expiry refunds | Capital lockup (stock must sit in a terminal) + fill risk + 5%-if-cancelled | Moderately expensive → hygiene only |
| T5 | **Far-room honeypot** — attractive buy order far away to make victims burn transfer energy | Dealer (victim) pays `ceil(amount×(1−e^(−d/30)))` energy + cooldown (`:339`) | 5% fee (refunded if unfilled); if filled, attacker genuinely pays credits for the goods | Expensive-ish, and Ibex **already nets energy out** (`ordersystem.rs:256-260`) → hygiene: hard cost cap + min margin + fix the amount-mismatch defect |
| T6 | **Stale-quote pickoff** — trade into our unrepriced resting orders after a market move | Legitimate trading; zero attacker cost | 0 | **FREE → defend: order lifecycle (reprice/de-back cadence), small blocks** |
| T7 | **Distance front-running** — pre-positioned terminals win same-tick deal races (`:289`) | Structural; intershard deals are shuffled instead (`:400`) | Pre-positioning cost | We lose the race, not credits (partial fills clip cleanly) → hygiene: prefer maker; don't chase |
| T8 | **Intel harvesting** — our orders are public and room-tagged; a buy order for XGHO2 telegraphs defense prep | Order book is anonymous *to* us but our orders carry `roomName` | 0 | FREE → policy: strategic purchases prefer taker mode; sells of war materiel embargoed in wartime (below) |

**Conclusion:** the cheap attacks (T1/T2/T3/T6/T8) all exploit the same three gaps — *single-day price reference, depth-trusting plans, immortal quotes*. The expensive attacks need no new machinery. The design below spends its complexity exactly there.

## Decision

Adopt a **snapshot → pure planner → bounded executor** trading architecture with a manipulation-resistant value oracle, hard exposure caps, and a kill-switch. The `OrderQueue` request API (missions file buy/sell requests; `ordersystem.rs:56-76`) remains the producer seam — what changes is everything between the request and the intent.

```
TerminalMission / ReagentPlanner (0010) / crisis detectors     — demand & supply requests (unchanged seam)
        │
        ▼
MarketSnapshot (per cadence): book extracts, history, my orders, credits,
        terminal states, transactions tail, risk ledger
        │
        ▼
TradePlanner — PURE function: snapshot → Vec<TradeAction>          ← host-target tested (0006)
        │     {PlaceOrder, RepriceDown, DeBack, Deal, Halt}
        ▼
Executor — applies caps, files intents, updates risk ledger, emits seg-57 telemetry
```

### 1. FairValue oracle — the price model (kills T1/T2)

`fair_value(resource) -> Option<Credits>` is a pure function of the history window + chain anchors. No trading decision may reference a raw `.last()` day.

- **Trailing median, not latest mean.** Median of per-day `avg_price` over the full returned history window (up to 14 days), **volume-filtered**: days with volume < 10% of the window's median volume are dropped before taking the median. A single painted day cannot move a 14-day median; the attacker must paint ≥ 7 days of plausible volume — turning T1's one-evening cost into a multi-week sunk-fee campaign at ≥ organic volume, with the fee sunk every painted day.
- **Deviation gate (the IBEX-018 recommendation, made precise).** If the latest day's `avg` deviates from the trailing median by > 50%, or its volume z-score (vs the window) exceeds 3, the day is flagged anomalous: pricing uses the median, the anomaly counter increments (feeds §5 kill-switch), and the resource is barred from *taker* action that cadence.
- **Cross-resource value anchors (chain arithmetic from ADR 0010).** Energy is the numéraire. Reactions are 1+1→1 (`run-reaction.js:12-16`), so `chain_value(compound) = Σ chain_value(components) + lab premium`; factory level-0 gives `value(bar) ≈ 5·value(mineral) + 2·value(energy)` and `value(battery) ≤ 12·value(energy)` (lossy 600→50→500 round trip; `constants.js:1144-1286`). `fair_value = clamp(median_history, 0.5×chain_value, 2.0×chain_value)` for any resource with a decomposition. A manipulated single-resource history now *contradicts its own components*: to move XGHO2's clamp the attacker must also paint G, H, O, X **and energy** — and energy's organic volume makes painting it economically absurd.
- **Liquidity floor.** If the window's median daily volume < 5× our intended daily trade in that resource, history is untrusted entirely: price from anchors only, maker-only, half-size blocks.
- **Replaces** `can_trust_history` (`ordersystem.rs:349-351`) and closes the TODOs at `:367/:446`. The stddev check is demoted to one input among several — it can no longer be satisfied *by* the attack (T1's uniform wash passes it; the median window does not care).
- **Oracle input caveat.** The oracle's entire input is `getHistory`, which is backend/storage-side aggregation, **not** pinned engine code (engine-mechanics.md verify-list #6) — its window/shape is unverified on a private server. Fixture tests (M0) **plus the M4 adversary scenario are therefore REQUIRED before any live buy flag flips** (M4 is upgraded from optional to a gate on M2's flag flip — see Migration).

### 2. Exposure caps — bound the worst case even if the oracle is fooled

All enforced in the Executor, fed by the **risk ledger** (per-day rolling counters: credits spent/received, units per resource, fills, realized slippage):

- **Per-deal:** ≤ 1% of current credits; per-order block stays 2,000 units (the one good cap that exists today).
- **Per-day:** buy spend ≤ 5% of credits above `credit_reserve`; per-resource traded volume ≤ 10,000 units (≈ 5 blocks) and ≤ 2× that resource's trailing median *organic* daily volume share we could plausibly absorb without becoming the market.
- **Price ceilings/floors:** buys ≤ 1.2× fair_value (2.0× under declared energy-crisis or war-urgency, §6); sells ≥ 0.8× fair_value. Maker buys bid **below** fair (fair − 5%), fixing the +0.1σ premium inversion.
- **Order budget:** ≤ 2 live orders per (room, resource), ≤ 50 total (engine cap 300, `game\market.js:90-93`) — slots are also a 30-day-expiry resource.
- Worst case with a fully-fooled oracle: daily-loss ≤ 5% of above-reserve credits × the ceiling overpay — survivable by construction, and the kill-switch (§5) trips well before the cap exhausts.

### 3. Transfer-cost discipline — prefer-local + hard ceilings (closes the "no upper bound" gap)

- **Market deals:** hard cap `cost_per_unit ≤ 0.25` (d ≈ 8.6 rooms) on any deal we initiate, *in addition to* netting energy at `fair_value(energy)` (not the manipulable latest-day avg the active path uses at `ordersystem.rs:419-420`). Energy buys via taker are self-defeating at distance (we pay energy to receive energy — at d=30 the net is 37%): cap `cost_per_unit ≤ 0.2` and **prefer a maker buy order in the deficit room**, which makes the *seller* pay transfer energy and cooldown (engine: dealer pays, `market.js:339`) for our 5% fee + a premium that compensates their haul.
- **Intra-empire sends:** the ratio-ranked terminal-delivery path gains a **hard floor**: reject any delivery with `cost_per_unit > 0.5` and prefer-local ordering (nearest eligible target wins ties) — fixing the unfloored `value = resources/cost` ranking at `transfersystem.rs:2104` flagged in IBEX-018's impact. Long-haul energy moves route through ADR 0010's battery compression instead (12:1).
- **Fix the amount mismatch:** deal `transferable_units`, not `transfer_amount` (`ordersystem.rs:282` vs `:262-267`).

### 4. Order lifecycle — maker/taker economics and quote hygiene (kills T6, starves T5)

- **Maker vs taker rule** (engine-derived): taking pays `frac(d)·fair_value(energy)` per unit + a cooldown slot; making pays the 5% fee but externalizes energy + cooldown to the counterparty (`market.js:339`). **Take iff** `net_taker_price` beats `maker_price × 0.95` by the margin parameter *and* the T5 cost cap passes; otherwise make. Sub-2k lots are always maker (deal minimums waste cooldowns).
- **Reprice cadence (~200 ticks over our resting orders):** sells chase the market **downward for free** (engine: downward reprice is free, `market.js:183-211`) toward `max(0.8×fair, best_ask − ε)`; **never reprice upward** (5% on the delta) — replace-by-expiry instead.
- **De-back, don't cancel — a sell-side trick.** Cancel forfeits the remaining fee (`:502-505`); expiry refunds `remainingAmount × order.price × 0.05` **at the order's price as it stands at expiry** (`market.js:507-508`, verified). An unwanted **sell** order is killed by hauling its backing stock out of the terminal — display amount drops to 0 (`:550-553`), it can never fill, and the full remaining fee comes back at 30 days because the price was never touched. The buy side has **no free reprice-to-never-fill analog**: flooring a buy order's price recomputes the eventual refund at the floored price, forfeiting nearly the whole fee — economically equivalent to canceling. The buy-side de-back instead works through the order's *backing*: buy display amount is recomputed every tick from the owner's credits and the destination terminal's free space (`:569-587`), so filling that terminal (or letting earmarked credits fall) zeroes the order with price — and refund — intact; where that is impractical, accept the loss. `cancel_order` is reserved for emergencies (kill-switch resource-exit) and the existing remaining==0 cleanup (`ordersystem.rs:340-344`).
- **Depth is not liquidity (T3):** the planner sizes *plans* (e.g. "this campaign's XZH2O will be bought") against fair_value and our budget only — never against displayed book depth. Resting counterparty orders inform *this cadence's* actions only, and a zero-clipped deal is treated as expected noise, not an error.
- **Self-trade guard:** never deal into our own orders (`ordersystem.rs:249` already filters `my_orders` — keep), and the planner asserts no same-tick place-and-take on the same resource, so we cannot accidentally paint our own history.

### 5. Risk controls — anomaly detection, kill-switch, telemetry

- **Anomaly score per resource:** deviation-gate hits (§1), volume z-score, realized slippage (fill price vs fair_value at fill time, from the engine transactions log) > 10%, and crossed-book sanity (best bid ≥ best ask on a resource we trade = manipulation in progress).
- **Counterparty graylist (post-trade):** the order book is anonymous pre-trade, but `incoming/outgoingTransactions` expose the counterparty and order id. Players whose fills repeatedly coincide with anomalies are graylisted; orders in rooms known (via [0009](0009-room-planning-and-multiroom-layout.md)/[0008](0008-combat-and-squad-architecture.md) intel) to belong to graylisted or at-war players are excluded from taker action. **Wartime embargo:** maker sells cannot choose counterparties, so resources on the war-materiel list (boost compounds, energy, Ghodium) are simply **not offered** while [0008](0008-combat-and-squad-architecture.md) reports an active conflict — selling XGHO2 to the player sieging us is not a trade, it is logistics for the enemy.
- **Kill-switch — `TradeGovernor: Normal | Restricted | Halted`.** Triggers: aggregate anomaly score breach, daily-loss cap ≥ 80% consumed, risk-ledger deserialization failure, or operator flag. **Restricted** = maker-only, half caps. **Halted** = no new orders or deals; resting sells de-backed; holds for one full history day, then re-enters Restricted. State transitions and reasons are emitted to the **seg-57 metrics block** ([0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md)): `{credits, daily_spend, daily_proceeds, fills, slippage_bps, anomaly_flags[], governor_state, reason}` — the operator's alert channel, decoupled from the debug flag per 0004's telemetry doctrine.
- **Everything is host-testable.** FairValue, TradePlanner, caps, and the governor transition function are pure functions over `MarketSnapshot` fixtures ([0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md)). The adversarial fixture set encodes the threat table: a painted-history day (T1), a thin-book spike sequence (T2), a hollow-wall snapshot (T3), a far honeypot (T5), a stale-quote drift (T6). Assertions: no buy above ceiling, no deal above the cost cap, kill-switch trips on T1/T2 fixtures, plans never reference displayed depth. A second-account **market-adversary script** on the private server gives an end-to-end version of the same fixtures.
- **Ledger persistence:** daily counters rebuild approximately from the engine transactions tail (last 100 each way) after a VM reset; a small serialized rolling block (labelled segment, `serde(default)` per [0002](0002-serialization.md)) carries exact counters + governor state across resets.

### 6. Buy programs (what we buy and why)

1. **War minerals (ADR 0010 L3, the largest customer).** ReagentPlanner deficits (Catalyst, missing bases, slow-chain intermediates — XZH2O's 160t chain makes buying it explicitly sanctioned by 0010 Tier C) flow through `request_passive_purchase`/taker per the §4 rule, at ≤ 1.2× fair (war-urgency 2.0×), under §2 caps. Extends `can_purchase_resource` (`terminal.rs:86-98`) beyond the current 7 bases + energy; `buy_minerals` flips on here (0010 L3 already plans this).
2. **Energy crisis imports (with ADR 0011).** Trigger: room storage energy below a crisis floor with spawn throughput at risk. Action: **maker buy order in the crisis room** at `fair × (1 + premium)` (seller pays transfer; we pay fee + premium — strictly better than taker for energy, §3), crisis ceiling 2.0× fair, funded from the reserve (this is what the reserve is *for*).
3. **Not bought:** intershard items (CPU unlock, subscription tokens, pixels, access keys) — same posture as 0010's `OPERATE_FACTORY` invariant: **no automated intershard purchase until a deliberate, recorded operator decision**; the credit math (one-off, irreversible, account-scoped) does not belong to an autonomous loop yet.

### 7. Sell programs (what we sell and in what form)

- **Tiered excess** keeps `TerminalMission`'s threshold structure (`terminal.rs:100-126` — it is sound); pricing and lifecycle change per §1/§4. Passive blocks stay 2,000.
- **Raw vs processed (ADR 0010 L4 gate):** sell bars instead of raw mineral only when `median(bar) > 5·median(mineral) + 2·median(energy) + margin`, evaluated with §1 medians; energy leaves the empire only as batteries or via near, high-margin orders (§3 caps make distant raw-energy sales unprofitable by rule, matching the engine's cost curve).
- **Compound surplus** above 0010's tier targets is sellable **except** the war-materiel embargo list during conflict (§5).

### 8. Credit strategy — what credits are FOR

Credits earn nothing and buy nothing structural; they are a **buffer and a procurement instrument**, in priority order: (1) **survival reserve** = `credit_reserve` (keep the existing 10M default, `features.rs:148`) — earmarked for crisis energy (§6.2) and Tier-A defense-boost imports (0010), never for economy buys; (2) **war chest** = planned campaigns × boost-import cost × 1.5 (sized from 0008's declared objectives, mirroring 0010 Tier C); (3) **growth budget** = the §2 daily allowance for 0010 L3 imports; (4) **surplus above all three converts to assets** (minerals, power) — held resources compound through labs/GCL; held credits do not. Target shape: reserve floor inviolate, war chest replenished before growth resumes.

### 9. CPU posture (per [0004](0004-cpu-governance-and-load-shedding.md))

The expensive operation is `get_all_orders` deserialization, already cached per-resource per pass (`ordersystem.rs:306-322` — keep) and only fetched for resources with live requests. Cadence stays ~20 ticks (planner full pass), reprice pass ~200. Governor mapping: **Normal** full; **Conserve** stretch cadence ×5 and skip book fetches (maker-only from history); **Critical** suspend trading entirely **except** crisis-energy maker orders, which are book-free and O(1) intents — that Critical-tier exception is a **proposed** entry in [0004](0004-cpu-governance-and-load-shedding.md)'s shed-tier registry (pending 0004 sign-off; additions to that registry require amending 0004). The existing `can_execute_cpu(CpuBar::HighPriority)` site (`ordersystem.rs:333`) is exactly where the governor read lands. Trading is never survival-critical except §6.2, and §6.2 is deliberately the cheapest possible action.

## Alternatives Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Snapshot → pure planner + median/anchor oracle + caps + kill-switch (chosen)** | Defends exactly the cheap attacks (T1/T2/T3/T6/T8); worst case bounded by caps even if the oracle is fooled; pure-function core is fixture-testable per 0006; rides the existing OrderQueue seam | New tunables (window, caps, ceilings); anchors need 0010's chain math; slightly more conservative fills (median lags real regime changes by ~W/2 days) |
| Patch `can_trust_history` only (add trailing-median check, keep everything else) | Tiny diff; direct IBEX-018 closure | No exposure bound (a fooled gate still spends freely), no lifecycle (T6 untouched), no kill-switch; leaves the +0.1σ buy inversion and the deal-amount bug; this *is* the M1 quick-win below, insufficient as the end state |
| Volume-weighted mean (VWAP) over the window instead of median | Standard finance practice; uses all data | A single high-volume painted day *dominates* a VWAP — it re-opens T1 with one parameter; median + volume-filter is strictly more robust to the actual threat |
| Per-counterparty pre-trade screening as the primary defense | Targets the attacker, not the symptom | The order book is anonymous pre-trade (engine fact); only post-trade transactions reveal counterparties — screening can only ever be the §5 *complement*, not the foundation |
| Don't trade at all (keep flags off permanently) | Zero attack surface | Cedes ADR 0010's import-dependent boost floor (Catalyst-less empires never reach Tier A), crisis energy, and the surplus-conversion sink; the review's "no empire-level resource allocation" verdict stays open; abstinence is a posture, not a design |
| Arbitrage/market-making profit engine (quote both sides, harvest spread) | Top-bot lore (SteveTrov utrium arbitrage); credits compound | Requires beating pre-positioned dealers on distance (T7 structurally favors incumbents), deep tuning, and continuous attention; profit-seeking widens the attack surface this ADR exists to shrink. Out of scope until the defensive system has eval history |

## Consequences

**Positive**
- **IBEX-018 closed with margins, not vibes:** every cheap attack in the threat table is either made ≥ an order of magnitude more expensive (T1: one painted day → ≥ 7 sustained days against a median that anchors also clamp) or made irrelevant to our decisions (T3/T6/T8 by construction). The expensive attacks get hygiene, not machinery — complexity is spent where the attacker's cost is low.
- **Bounded worst case:** even a fully-fooled oracle cannot lose more than the daily cap; the kill-switch turns a sustained anomaly into a loud, telemetered halt instead of a slow bleed ([0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md) alerting).
- **ADR 0010 unblocked safely:** the boost pipeline's market dependence (its stated "new risk") now has the trust-guarded path it assumed; crisis energy protects ADR 0011's spawn uptime; intra-empire sends get the cost floor the review asked for.
- **Engine-economics literacy banked:** maker-externalizes-cost, de-back-don't-cancel, downward-reprice-free, expiry-refund — each is a small standing edge over players running folklore models (e.g. "cancel to get the fee back", which is backwards).
- **Fully fixture-testable core** — the trading brain runs on the host against adversarial snapshots before it ever touches credits.

**Negative / costs**
- A real tunables surface (window, caps, ceilings, margins, cadences) — config-driven and eval-diffable, but it *will* need tuning passes; the median lag means we under-react to genuine regime shifts by design (we forgo some upside to be unmanipulable).
- More conservative trading earns fewer credits than an aggressive tuned trader — accepted: this ADR optimizes for *not losing*, per the operator's ask.
- The anchors couple this ADR to 0010's chain-math kernel (shared code, shipped at 0010 L0).

**New risks**
- **Over-halting:** a volatile-but-honest market day can trip the kill-switch and idle trading for a day. Acceptable by design (trading is never survival-critical; §6.2 stays alive under Restricted), and the fixture suite includes an honest-volatility snapshot asserting Normal is retained.
- **Ledger drift after reset:** the transactions-tail rebuild is approximate beyond 100 entries; the serialized counter block bounds the error, and the governor fails toward Restricted (safe direction) on ledger deser failure.
- **Embargo leakage:** war materiel can still exit via pre-war resting orders — the wartime transition de-backs them (§4 mechanics make this free).

**CPU / tick-safety.** Net neutral-to-positive: same cadence structure as today, book fetches become rarer (Conserve skips them), intents remain trivial. No new panic surfaces: the executor returns `Result` per [0005](0005-runtime-and-scheduling-model.md) discipline; all JS-boundary reads (orders, history, transactions) are snapshot-copied before the pure planner runs.

## Incremental Migration Path

Stable seams: **the `OrderQueue` request API** (producers unchanged), **`MarketSnapshot`/`TradePlanner`** (new, pure), and the **seg-57 metrics block** ([0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md)). Per the rewrite plan §3, the full system is **Increment 7** ("market trailing-window trust"); the IBEX-018 hardening rides **Increments 1–2 as quick-wins** on the existing `ordersystem.rs` (plan §3's quick-win mechanism / proposed-fixes backlog). Never break the running bot mid-increment — the existing order system keeps running until M2 swaps its brain.

1. **M0 — Kernels & fixtures (after Inc 0; Breaking: None).** `MarketSnapshot` type + FairValue oracle (median, volume filter, deviation gate, anchor clamp via 0010 L0's chain-math kernel) + governor transition function as pure kernels; the adversarial fixture set (T1/T2/T3/T5/T6 + honest-volatility control). Nothing wired; flags stay off. **Validate:** kernel tests green; painted-day fixture moves the latest-day mean ≥ 10× while the oracle's output moves < 5%.
2. **M1 — IBEX-018 quick-win hardening (rides Inc 1–2; Breaking: Behavioral).** On the existing system: (a) replace `can_trust_history`'s `.last()` gate with the oracle (closes `ordersystem.rs:349-351, :367, :446`); (b) maker buys priced below fair (fix the +0.1σ inversion at `:458`); (c) deal `transferable_units` not `transfer_amount` (`:282`); (d) hard `cost_per_unit` caps on active sells and the **floor on the intra-empire ratio path** (`transfersystem.rs:2104`); (e) ephemeral daily-spend counter (transactions-tail derived — **no new serialized state**) enforcing the §2 caps. **Validate (harness + fixtures):** painted-day fixture produces zero buy actions; far-honeypot fixture produces zero deals above the cost cap; smoke-run shows unchanged sell throughput on the honest scenario.

   **M0+M1 LANDED 2026-06-12, with (e) operator-amended.** (a)–(d) as specified: the oracle is `transfer/fairvalue.rs` (median + volume filter + deviation/z gates + liquidity floor; the **anchor clamp awaits 0010 L0's chain-math kernel and stays M2** — until then untrusted history means abstain, since maker-only-from-anchors is not yet possible); adversarial fixtures landed host-side (T1 painted-day moves the oracle < 5% and is flagged; T2 volume spike; honest-volatility control; thin-market floor). (e) was **amended by operator direction**: instead of an ephemeral counter, the exposure ledger **and** the per-resource history-day cache persist in the dedicated **seg 58** (`MARKET_SEGMENT`) — M3's segment pulled forward in interim form, carrying its own `MARKET_MEMORY_VERSION`, deliberately decoupled from the component segments' `WORLD_FORMAT_VERSION`. Rationale: an ephemeral ledger lets a VM reset double the daily caps, and the stored history window keeps the oracle's input durable across resets and backend gaps (`getHistory` is backend-side and sparse on a private server). Mechanics honor the engine's shared 10-segments-touched budget (engine-mechanics §9.1; the steady-state active set is 10 of 10, so there is no free write slot): read once per VM lifetime via request-until-loaded (trading gates on `loaded`); writes go through the arbiter's queued-write path, which lands them end-of-tick when they fit and otherwise reserves a next-tick key slot by displacing the lowest-priority active id (live stats) for one tick. M3's remaining scope (TradeGovernor state, graylist, kill-switch telemetry, slippage from the transactions tail) lands on the same block.
3. **M2 — Full TradePlanner (Inc 7, with 0010 L3/L4; Breaking: Behavioral).** Snapshot→planner→executor swap behind the OrderQueue seam; maker/taker rule; order-lifecycle pass (reprice-down / de-back / expiry accounting); buy programs wired to the ReagentPlanner and crisis detector; raw-vs-processed sell gate; wartime embargo read from 0008; flip `market.sell` defaults **on** under the governor and `credit_reserve` — the **buy-flag flips are gated on M4 passing** (the `getHistory` caveat, §1). **Validate:** harness Catalyst-less-empire scenario reaches 0010's Tier A floor via purchases within caps; lifecycle test shows a stale sell order repriced down within one reprice cadence and an unwanted order de-backed (never cancelled); intent-diff on the honest scenario shows no regression in fill rate.
4. **M3 — Risk ledger persistence + kill-switch + telemetry (Inc 7; Breaking: Memory/format).** Serialized rolling counter block + governor state in a labelled segment (**seg 58, pinned in [0002](0002-serialization.md)'s segment allocation registry**; `serde(default)`, [0002](0002-serialization.md) discipline — additive, no reset required); seg-57 market metrics block; counterparty graylist fed by transactions + room-ownership intel. **Validate:** forced VM reset preserves exact counters; T1 fixture trips Halted within one cadence and emits the alert; honest-volatility fixture does not.
5. **M4 (REQUIRED before M2's live buy-flag flip) — adversary-bot harness scenario (after M3; Breaking: None).** Second account on the private server runs the wash-paint script end-to-end; assert the live bot halts and the operator alert fires. **Required, not optional:** the FairValue oracle's `getHistory` input is backend-side and unpinned (§1 caveat; engine-mechanics verify-list #6), so fixture-level coverage alone cannot certify it — `market.buy*` defaults stay **off** until this scenario passes.

**Breaking-change summary:** M0 — **None**. M1/M2 — **Behavioral** (what gets priced/bought/sold changes; no serialized field added or reordered; flag-default flips are config). M3 — **Memory/format** (one additive labelled segment; no state drop, no reset — does not consume either of the plan's two sanctioned reset windows). M4 — **None**. No step touches `serialize_world`'s frozen seam, and the running bot's trading never stops mid-increment (M2's swap is flag-gated with the old path as fallback for one validation window).
