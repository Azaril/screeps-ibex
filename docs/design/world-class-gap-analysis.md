## 1. THE TARGET — the dominant bot as a system-of-systems

The engine fixes the win condition's arithmetic. Every dominant bot is, structurally, the same seven engines compounding into each other; what differs is execution quality. The flow, with each engine's objective function:

```
            ┌────────────── INTEL BRAIN (max: decision quality per CPU) ──────────────┐
            │ observers · scouts · player profiles · threat & opportunity scoring     │
            ▼                          ▼                          ▼
  ECONOMY ENGINE            EXPANSION ENGINE             MILITARY DETERRENT
  (max energy/tick per      (max GCL→claimed-room        (min expected loss; make
   CPU and per spawn-tick)   conversion rate)             attacking us -EV)
            │ surplus energy + minerals     │ rooms = more engines      │ safety
            ▼                               ▼                           ▼
  RESOURCE PIPELINE (0010)       SPAWN THROUGHPUT (0011)       MARKET ARM (0012)
  (max boost/commodity stock     (max useful part-ticks        (convert surplus ↔
   per lab-tick)                  per lane-tick)                credits at min risk)
            └────────────→ SELF-IMPROVEMENT LOOP (0006: max score-gain per deploy) ←──┘
```

**The economy engine** maximizes energy/tick per CPU and per spawn-tick. The ceilings are hard: an owned source yields 3000 energy per 300-tick regen = **10 e/t** (`sources\tick.js:46-59`, `ENERGY_REGEN_TIME=300`), so an owned room's two sources are 20 e/t; a *reserved* remote source is also 3000-cap (10 e/t gross, minus hauler/reserver/road overhead — net ~5–7 e/t at typical distances); an SK-room source is **4000-cap (13.3 e/t) and there are three per room plus a mineral with prebuilt extractor** (`sources\tick.js:46-59`; prior-art digest §4 SK farming). A mature room therefore runs ~20 e/t native + 30–50 e/t net from ~6 remotes (KasamiBot's number) + optionally ~40 e/t gross from an SK room. The dominant-bot discipline is **per-source P&L** (Winsley's "franchise" accounting): every remote is a profit center whose net income, not gross, is the budget all spending draws from. Ibex's mining tree is already its most mature subsystem (review §2-#4) — the gap is the accounting, not the harvesting.

**The spawn-throughput engine** is the binding constraint the economy feeds. One spawn = 1 creep at a time, 3 ticks/part (`spawns\create-creep.js:36,48`), so a room is capped at **0.33/0.67/1.0 parts/tick (RCL≤6/7/8) = 500/1000/1500 sustained living parts** — and prior art is unanimous that a mature bot is *spawn-blocked*, not energy-blocked (digest §5.1; [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) Context). The single largest force multiplier in the entire game follows directly: **`PWR_OPERATE_SPAWN` at level 5 multiplies spawn time ×0.2 (`create-creep.js:51-54`), turning an RCL8 room's 1.0 parts/tick into 5.0 — a 7,500-sustained-part room**. A power-creep-running empire fields five times the army and economy per room of one that ignores power. That is not an end-game garnish; it is the difference in kind between tiers.

**The expansion engine** converts surplus into GCL and GCL into rooms. GCL level n→n+1 costs `1,000,000 × n^2.4` (`utils.js:661-663`) — brutally superlinear, so *GCL efficiency* matters more than raw energy: at RCL8 the upgrade cap is 15 e/t room-wide, **but GCL credits the boosted amount** (`upgradeController.js:42-52,84-86`) — XGH2O-boosted upgraders earn **30 GCL/t at the same 15 e/t**, and `PWR_OPERATE_CONTROLLER` lifts the cap to +50 for **130 GCL/t with a level-5 operator** (engine digest). Dominant players add GCL *temples* (a dedicated room pumped by terminal, upgraded boosted, unclaimed and re-claimed to repeat — prior-art digest §4). The expansion decision itself is an investment comparison the engine makes precise: a claimed room's ~20 e/t native income (plus remotes) compounds forever, versus 15–30 GCL/t of upgrading — which is why top bots expand aggressively early and temple late. Route-aware claiming + incubation (a mature room spawning/feeding the newborn) is what makes the conversion *fast*; both are already Increment-7 work ([0009](0009-room-planning-and-multiroom-layout.md) D3, [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) D5.3).

**The military deterrent** exists to make attacking us negative-EV, and only secondarily to take rooms. The engine is lopsidedly pro-defender: repair is ~100 hits/energy, an RCL8 room can repair ~60k hits/tick, and "an attacker needs ≥3× the defender's investment" (Tigga, prior-art digest §2); safe mode (20k ticks, 50k cooldown, `constants.js:242-244`) resets any siege. Six towers at optimal range deal 3,600/tick but a 4-creep boosted quad pools 4,800 hp/tick of cross-heal (4×25 HEAL ×48) — **so quads beat towers and only creeps + spike coordination beat quads** (combat digest §"Quad sustain"). The tick-snapshot model (`main.js:32-66`: all code runs against tick-start state, all intents resolve together) makes everything **predictive**: pre-heal before damage lands (damage applies before heal before the death check, `creeps\tick.js:118-135`), pre-position, pre-spawn, pre-stock boosts. The doctrine ranking for a dominant bot: (1) defense-first — ramparts + melee defenders *under* ramparts + heal-aware towers that refuse drain bait (Ibex's tower brain already computes `net_tower_damage`/`is_likely_tower_drain`, `military/damage.rs:88-111`, `missions/tower.rs:266-270` — a genuine strength); (2) cohesive boosted quads as the offense unit ([0003](0003-behavior-modeling.md)/[0008](0008-combat-and-squad-architecture.md)); (3) a **fully automated war decision loop** — scouting → target selection → strategy choice in code, the Tigga/SteveTrov line most bots never cross (digest §2); (4) nukes as the siege-breaker: 50k-tick flight, kills *every creep in the room* (ramparts don't save them), 10M/5M structure damage, cancels active safe mode, blocks upgrading 200 ticks (`nukes\tick.js:16-78`) — saturation-stacked and paired with an assault timed to landing.

**The resource pipeline** ([0010](0010-boost-lab-factory-pipeline.md)) is the war chest and the GCL doubler: always-on lab production into tiered stockpiles (defense floor → XGH2O → offense → G), because the chain math forbids reactive brewing (one boosted quad member ≈ 3,150 full-cluster lab-ticks — 0010 §"arithmetic"). **The market arm** (ADR 0012, companion in this pass) converts between surplus and credits under manipulation guards (IBEX-018), buys missing reagents, and sells compressed surplus — with the engine's sharp edges respected (5% fee sunk on cancel, distance-priority deal races, the dealer always pays energy + cooldown; econ digest §3). **The intel brain** feeds all of it: observers are *free* RCL8 scouting (one intent, no creep), scouts fill the rest, and a persistent per-player profile (army size, boost tier, aggression history, rebuild speed) is what turns war from per-room reaction into empire-level target selection. **The self-improvement loop** ([0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md)) is the meta-engine: dominant players iterate on dashboards (Winsley's Grafana-driven 11→4 CPU/room rewrite); the harness + seg-57 metrics + colony-health diffing is exactly that loop, and §4 below tightens its objective to measure dominance, not just health.

**CPU is the universal currency.** Every engine above is denominated in CPU (execution + ~0.2/intent), and the competitive band is ~2–10 CPU per owned room including remotes (digest §4 CPU benchmarks). The global `CpuGovernor` + budgeted pathfinding facade ([0004](0004-cpu-governance-and-load-shedding.md)) is therefore not just survival — it is the *pricing mechanism* that lets the empire scale rooms until CPU, not GCL, is the binding constraint, then spend bucket surplus on pixels (MMO) once nothing better exists.

## 4. OBJECTIVE-FUNCTION CHECK — does colony-health measure dominance?

The four-term colony-health score (plan §1, [0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md) §(2): survival-gated, CPU headroom, economic growth, military win-rate) is the right *survival and regression* function — keep it untouched as the pre-deploy gate. But it under-measures dominance in four specific ways, and a bot optimized only against it would plateau exactly where the prior-art digest says bots plateau:

1. **It measures growth, not efficiency.** GCL slope rewards raw energy spend; it cannot distinguish unboosted 15 GCL/t from boosted 30 GCL/t at the same energy, nor a 10-CPU room from a 4-CPU room at equal output. **Add two ratio sub-metrics to the ECONOMIC term:** `energy_throughput / cpu_used` (incl. intents — the §5.1-of-prior-art discipline; the digest's competitive band is 2–10 CPU/room) and `gcl_per_upgrade_energy` (target 2.0 at RCL8 once G-7a lands).
2. **It cannot see the binding constraint.** Spawn-blockedness is invisible to all four terms until it manifests as slowed growth. **Promote [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md)'s seg-57 spawn-uptime KPI into the score:** `spawn_utilization %` (busy lane-ticks ÷ available lane-ticks) with its *interpretation* posture-dependent (high while growing = good; pegged at 100% with unmet Defense-class demand = an alarm). This is the metric that will later prove OPERATE_SPAWN's 5× claim (G-2c) empirically.
3. **It measures winning fights, not deterring them.** Military win-rate scores engagements that happened. Dominance is mostly fights that *don't* happen (Tigga's deterrence-by-reputation). **Add to the MILITARY term:** `boost_coverage %` (fraction of combat parts boosted in engagements + Tier-A floor satisfaction, from 0010), and a `deterrence_events` counter — hostile creeps sighted in owned/reserved rooms that withdrew without dealing damage, sieges abandoned pre-breach, and consecutive ticks of zero rooms lost. Imperfect proxies, but countable from data the threatmap already produces.
4. **It has no intel or market dimension.** A blind bot scores identically to an all-seeing one until the day it dies surprised. **Add two INFO-weight (low) terms:** `intel_freshness` (share of strategically-relevant rooms — own remotes + 2-range shell + tracked-player rooms — with intel age below threshold; data exists in `DynamicVisibilityData.updated_within`, `controllerbehavior.rs:37`) and, once ADR 0012 lands, `market_pnl` (credits delta normalized by volume, manipulation-guard violations counting negative).

Weighting rule: the new metrics enter as **sub-metrics of the existing four terms (plus the survival gate untouched)** rather than new top-level terms, so the score stays comparable across the Inc-0..7 baseline corpus; re-baseline once at the Inc-8 boundary with both scorings recorded. The harness caveat from 0006 §Non-goals extends here: private-server runs validate *behavior* of these metrics, not MMO absolutes (and G-15 is entirely invisible to the harness) — the MMO seg-57 stream is the source of truth for the dominance ratios.

**Bottom line.** Ibex plus its planned rewrite (Inc 0–7 + ADRs 0001–0012) reaches "healthy, expanding, defensible" — roughly The International's tier. The verified gaps between that and *dominant* concentrate in five absences: **power creeps (a 5× spawn multiplier with zero code today), the GCL boost consumer (a 2× GCL multiplier whose producer is already designed in 0010), empire-level intel/player profiling (the prerequisite for automated war), offensive nukes (the only siege-breaker the engine's defender-favoring math respects), and SK-room economics (the largest untapped energy block, where the combat half already exists)**. None require re-deciding anything in ADRs 0001–0012; all compose onto their seams. Increment 8 as proposed above is where Ibex stops catching up and starts compounding.
