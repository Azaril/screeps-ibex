## Context

### Why power wins — the dominance flywheel, quantified from the engine

Every term below is an engine constant or a one-step derivation; nothing is aspirational. The flywheel:

```
cohesive squads (Inc 4 / 0008)──→ highway bank farming ──→ power ──→ processing (50 e/unit) ──→ GPL
                                                                                                 │
   bigger army + bigger economy ←── operator powers ←── operators (create/level cost GPL) ←──────┘
```

1. **`PWR_OPERATE_SPAWN` is the largest single multiplier in the game.** Spawn time ×[0.9, 0.7, 0.5, 0.35, **0.2**] by rank (`common\constants.js:861-868`; applied at `engine\processor\intents\spawns\create-creep.js:51-54`). [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) proves a mature room is **spawn-blocked** at 1.0 parts/tick (RCL8); rank 5 turns that into **5.0 parts/tick → 7,500 sustained living parts per room** (mechanics ref §3.1). Everything else the bot fields — economy and war alike — is denominated in this resource.
2. **`PWR_REGEN_SOURCE` out-earns the source itself.** +[50…250] energy every 15 ticks for 300 ticks (`constants.js:955-963`; applied in `engine\processor\intents\sources\tick.js:31-39`, capped at `energyCapacity` so miners must keep the source drained). Rank 5 = +5,000 energy per cast = **+16.7 e/t per source** on top of the natural 10 e/t — and it costs **zero ops** (no `ops` field in its POWER_INFO entry). Two sources → +33 e/t per room, more than an entire extra owned room's native income.
3. **`PWR_OPERATE_CONTROLLER` breaks the RCL8 ceiling.** +[10…50] to the 15 e/t room-wide upgrade cap (`constants.js:999-1007`); with XGH2O (×2 GCL per energy at the cap, [0010](0010-boost-lab-factory-pipeline.md) Tier B) a rank-5 effect yields **130 GCL/t versus the unboosted 15** — the GCL-temple engine.
4. **`PWR_OPERATE_LAB` triples the boost pipeline.** +[2…10] output per reaction on the base 5 (`constants.js:888-896`; `engine\processor\intents\labs\run-reaction.js:12-16`). 0010's bottleneck arithmetic (one boosted quad member ≈ 3,150 full-cluster ticks) divides by 3 → **~1,050 ticks**.
5. **`PWR_OPERATE_TOWER` re-crosses the anti-quad line.** Tower effect ×[1.1…1.5] (`constants.js:870-877`; applied pre-floor in `engine\processor\intents\towers\attack.js:40-45`, same in `heal.js`/`repair.js`). Six RCL8 towers at optimal range: 3,600 → **5,400 dmg/t, above the 4,800 hp/t a boosted quad pools** (mechanics ref §2.10) — towers alone deter quads again. Conversely `PWR_DISRUPT_TOWER` (×[0.9…0.5], range 50, **cooldown 0**, `constants.js:930-938`) hands the same line to the attacker.
6. **`PWR_OPERATE_EXTENSION` collapses refill latency.** Instantly refills extensions closest-first from a storage/terminal/factory/container, up to [20…100]% of total extension capacity, for **2 ops** (`constants.js:897-904`; `engine\processor\intents\power-creeps\usePower.js:104-132`) — at RCL8 that is up to 12,000 energy placed in one tick, eliminating the between-waves refill stall [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) D2 watches for.
7. **`PWR_OPERATE_FACTORY` gates an entire industry.** Leveled commodities require an active effect of exactly the factory's level on every produce, and the first use **permanently brands the factory** (`engine\processor\intents\factories\produce.js:21-23`; `usePower.js:259-274`) — the irreversible decision 0010 §6 deferred to this ADR.

A power-running empire is therefore not "the same bot plus a garnish": per RCL8 room it fields ~5× the parts, ~1.5–2.7× the energy, 3× the boosts, and up to 8.7× the GCL of a power-ignoring bot. That compounding is what the gap analysis means by Increment 8 being where Ibex "stops catching up and starts compounding."

**The price, equally quantified:** processing costs **50 energy per power** (`POWER_SPAWN_ENERGY_RATIO`, `constants.js:269`; `engine\processor\intents\power-spawns\process-power.js:22-27`) — a power spawn running at its base 1 power/tick drains 50 e/t, the full output of five sources. GPL is brutally superlinear: `GPL = floor(sqrt(total_processed / 1000))` (`engine\processor\global-intents\power\upgradePowerCreep.js:11` with `POWER_LEVEL_MULTIPLY=1000`, `POWER_LEVEL_POW=2`, `constants.js:808-809`), so **GPL n costs 1000·n² lifetime power = 50,000·n² energy**. The flywheel is a marathon, which is exactly why its cheap front half (bank farming, processing) must start increments before its expensive back half (operators) lands.

### What exists today (verified against code)

- **Bank targeting & scoring (G-2a, live with two defects).** `war.rs` scans visible rooms for banks (`operations/war.rs:691-694`), scores them (`:762-792`: `power ≥ 1000`, stored energy > 100k, `roi = power/8000`, distance penalty, decay bonus) and launches an `AttackOperation` with `TargetSource::PowerBank{power, ticks_to_decay}` (`war.rs:42`). Defect 1 — **IBEX-043**: the concurrency gate counts ALL active attacks (`war.rs:766-771`, the inline "we'll filter below" never happens), so `max_concurrent_power_banks` (= `min(2, room_count)`, `war.rs:949`) is consumed by unrelated wars. Defect 2 — **the feasibility window omits kill time**: `min_ticks_needed = dist×50 + 500` (`war.rs:765`) versus an actual unboosted kill of ~2,700–3,400 ticks (§D1.2 below) — the gate green-lights banks that will decay mid-kill.
- **Bank force plan (live, arithmetically broken).** `AttackReason::PowerBank` plans `SquadComposition::duo_melee_heal()` (`operations/attack.rs:185-191`) — the *generic* duo — while the purpose-built `power_bank_duo()` (`military/composition.rs:396-413`, `PowerBankAttacker`/`PowerBankHealer` bodies) has **zero call sites**. Worse, the dedicated bodies don't close their own loop: `power_bank_attacker_body` allows up to 25×ATTACK (750 dps → 375/t reflect; `military/bodies.rs:428-440`) while `power_bank_healer_body` caps at 25×HEAL = 300 hp/t (`bodies.rs:442-453`) — its own comment admits "needs ~32 HEAL" (`bodies.rs:443`), which is unreachable in a 50-part 1:1 HEAL/MOVE body. The healer cannot sustain the attacker the plan spawns. §D1.2 fixes the arithmetic.
- **Bank haulers (live).** Post-kill hauler waves sized by loot (`missions/attack_mission.rs:1400-1430`, `SquadComposition::power_bank_haulers`, `composition.rs:417-426`); bank power already counts as loot value (`attack_mission.rs:1582-1584`).
- **Processing (G-2b, live, unthrottled).** `PowerSpawnMission` (`missions/powerspawn.rs`, launched by `ColonyMission` when a power spawn exists, `missions/colony.rs:246-253`) registers haul deposits for energy+power (`powerspawn.rs:87-147`) and calls `process_power()` whenever `energy > 50 && power > 0` (`powerspawn.rs:166-173`). Two gaps: **no surplus throttle** (it drains 50 e/power regardless of room posture — the arbitration ADR 0014 owns), and the **refill priority map is inverted** (`powerspawn.rs:102-110`: free-fraction < 0.25 → High — i.e. topping-off a nearly-full spawn outranks refilling an empty one; harmless for the 100-cap power store, wasteful for the 5,000-cap energy store).
- **Plumbing (done).** `TransferTarget::PowerSpawn` end-to-end (`transfer/transfersystem.rs:113,144,163,209,247,416-419`); `power_banks()`/`power_spawns()` in room data (`room/data.rs:552-554, 702-707`); GPL level/progress already in stats (`statssystem.rs:211,234-238,312`); the foreman hub stamp places the power spawn ([0009](0009-room-planning-and-multiroom-layout.md)); observers already serve targeted visibility (`room/visibilitysystem.rs:428-513`, G-5).
- **Operators (G-2c): nothing.** Crate-wide `PowerCreep` search: **zero matches**. GPL accrues and is never spent. `screeps-game-api 0.23` (`screeps-ibex/Cargo.toml:35`) exposes the full power-creep API, so this is bot work, not dependency work.

### Engine ground truth — the power rulebook (all load-bearing claims cited)

**Use-gates** (`engine\processor\intents\power-creeps\usePower.js`): in any room **with a controller**, every power — including `GENERATE_OPS` — requires `controller.isPowerEnabled` (`:9-12`); a **non-owner** is additionally blocked while the room's **safe mode is active** (`:13-15`). Rooms without a controller (highways, SK/center rooms) have no gate. Per-power checks: rank > 0 and per-power cooldown (`:25`), ops in the creep's own store (`:29-37`), range (`:39-45`), and a **higher-rank still-active effect cannot be overwritten by a lower one** (`:47-50`). Effects: `endTime = now + duration[rank]`, replace-same-power (`:277-289`); the creep pays ops and starts `cooldownTime = now + cooldown` (`:291-298`). Power-creep intents have **no conflict matrix** — `move + usePower + withdraw + transfer` all in one tick is legal (`power-creeps\intents.js:3-12`) — but the intent store holds **one `usePower` per tick**.

**enableRoom** (`power-creeps\enableRoom.js`): any power creep, range ≤1 to any controller, sets `isPowerEnabled: true` (`:19`); blocked only when `target.user != object.user && safeMode active` (`:12-14`). **An enemy operator can therefore enable YOUR room** unless safe mode is up. The flag persists until controller downgrade-to-0 or unclaim (`controllers\tick.js:52-62,80`; `controllers\unclaim.js:26`) — effectively permanent for a healthy room.

**Lifecycle** (verified): account-level creeps minted by `createPowerCreep` — costs 1 GPL of allowance, level 0, 1,000 hits, 100 store (`global-intents\power\createPowerCreep.js:27-39`); the allowance check is `#creeps + Σlevels < GPL` (`createPowerCreep.js:11-13`, `upgradePowerCreep.js:11-13`). Each `upgradePowerCreep` adds +1 level, +1,000 hitsMax, +100 store, and +1 rank in one chosen power, gated by that power's `level[]` array (`upgradePowerCreep.js:41-48`); max creep level **25**, max power rank 5 (`constants.js:812`; `upgradePowerCreep.js:22,37`). Spawn at an own power spawn → `ageTime = now + POWER_CREEP_LIFE_TIME(5000)` (`spawnPowerCreep.js:30-39`; `constants.js:813`). **`renew` at a power spawn *or a power bank* resets the full 5,000** (`power-creeps\renew.js:9,20` — the bank option means a bank-escort operator self-renews in the field). Death (age `gameTime ≥ ageTime−1` or hits ≤ 0, checked in the **global** stage, `global-intents\power.js:30-36`) drops the store into a container/tombstone and sets `spawnCooldownTime = Date.now() + 8 real-time hours` (`_diePowerCreep.js:51-58`; `POWER_CREEP_SPAWN_COOLDOWN = 8*3600*1000`, `constants.js:810`). **Levels and powers are never lost**; delete (to re-spec) takes 24 real-time hours and is cancellable (`deletePowerCreep.js:24-27`; `constants.js:811`). Movement: power creeps always pass `canMove` — **no fatigue, 1 tile/tick on any terrain** (`movement.js:11-14`); they wear roads like a 100-part creep (`movement.js:215-219`).

**Power banks**: 2M hits, capacity 500–5,000, despawn at `decayTime` (+5,000 ticks from spawn) (`constants.js:259-264`; decay processed at `processor.js:421-426`). **Hit-back is 50% of ALL damage dealt to the bank — ranged included, not melee-only** (`_damage.js:35-37` applies before the attack-type branch). The destroyed bank leaves a **ruin holding the power, decaying in 500 ticks** (`structures\_destroy.js` — ruin gets `store`, `RUIN_DECAY 500`); live banks reject `withdraw` (`creeps\withdraw.js:32`).

**Processing**: 1 power + 50 energy per `process_power` intent-tick; `PWR_OPERATE_POWER` (creep level ≥10) raises it to +[1…5] extra power/tick, each still ×50 energy (`power-spawns\process-power.js:16-27`); requires RCL8 (`powerSpawn {8:1}`, `constants.js:224`; controller check at `process-power.js:12-14`). Processed power increments `user.power` → GPL (`process-power.js:36-38`).

**Hard constraints** (unchanged): tick-snapshot concurrency — all of the above is **predictive** play (effects must be standing *before* the tick they matter); single-threaded WASM; CPU = execution + ~0.2/intent (a full operator rotation is ≤40 casts per kilotick — noise); VM-reset resilience; creeps 1,500t / operators 5,000t with the renew/respawn rules above; strangler-fig increments.

## Decision

Adopt the power pillar in **two halves with different price tags**: (a) the **accrual half** — fix bank farming, throttle processing — rides existing increments nearly for free; (b) the **spending half** — an **`OperatorSystem`** owning power-creep creation, builds, assignment, scheduling, and the enable policy — is the **Increment 8 anchor**, gated on M7. All of it composes onto existing seams (0008 objectives, 0011 demands, 0007 transfers, 0010 tiers); nothing below re-decides a sibling ADR.

### D1 — The acquisition chain (banks → power → home storage)

**D1.1 Scouting.** Banks live only on highways and last 5,000 ticks. The existing observer queue (G-5) gains the already-planned idle **sweep patrol** over highway rooms within range; bank sightings write `(power, decayTime)` intel. No new mechanism — `war.rs:691-694` already consumes structure data when visibility exists.

**D1.2 Go/no-go and squad math (replaces the `war.rs:765` window and the broken duo).** Engine inputs: 2M hits, 50% reflect on *all* damage, decay window `D_remain`, ruin decay 500t after the kill.

- **Feasibility:** required dps `≥ 2,000,000 / (D_remain − travel − spawn_time − margin)`. The launch gate becomes `kill_time(dps) + dist×50 + spawn + 200 ≤ ticks_to_decay` — fixing the `+500` flat fudge.
- **Unboosted duo (the corrected composition):** healer is the binding part. Reflect = dps/2; an unboosted healer caps at 25 HEAL (50-part 1:1 body) = 300 hp/t → attacker caps at **20 ATTACK (600 dps, reflect 300)** — not the current 25. Kill time 3,334t: viable only on fresh banks (`D_remain ≥ ~3,900` at distance ~6 rooms). Cost ~10,100 energy + 90 parts = 270 spawn-ticks.
- **Two duos** (banks typically expose 2+ adjacent tiles): 1,200 dps → 1,667t kill — the default plan; comfortable window, ~540 spawn-ticks.
- **Boosted duo (post-0010 L2):** XUH2O 20×ATTACK = 2,400 dps; reflect 1,200 = exactly one XLHO2 25×HEAL healer (1,200 hp/t). Kill 834t — opens almost every sighted bank. Boost spend rides 0010 Tier C.
- **Concurrency:** fix IBEX-043 (count only `TargetSource::PowerBank` attacks against `max_concurrent_power_banks`) and retire the dead-code split: route the plan through `power_bank_duo()` with the 20-ATTACK cap (or delete it and parameterize `duo_melee_heal` — one site, [0008](0008-combat-and-squad-architecture.md)'s objective→composition mapping owns the choice).
- **ROI gate:** the squad+hauler energy (~12–25k) is **~5–9 e/power for a ≥2,750 bank — noise against the 50 e/power processing cost**. The real scarce inputs are spawn-ticks (~270/duo) and the decay clock; score accordingly (`power × spawn-tick-pressure-discount`, not `power/8000` energy ROI).

**D1.3 Hauling.** Capacity 500–5,000 → ≤100 CARRY parts = 2–4 haulers (the existing loot-scaled wave, `attack_mission.rs:1400-1430`, is right). Two timing rules become explicit [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) demand fields: haulers carry a **deadline = projected kill tick + 500** (the ruin window) and a **prespawn** keyed to projected kill (arrive at kill, not after). Long-haul return rides ordinary [0007](0007-hauling-logistics.md) routes; cross-empire consolidation to the processing room is a `TerminalMission` send (power is an ordinary resource).

**D1.4 Field renew.** The bank-escort operator (once operators exist, §D3) renews at the bank itself (`renew.js:9`) — bank trips never count against operator lifetime.

### D2 — Processing policy (the GPL throttle)

`PowerSpawnMission` is kept (it is correct plumbing) with two changes:

1. **Surplus gate:** `process_power` only when the room's stored energy exceeds the posture threshold — concretely, the same surplus tier 0010 L2 uses (default: storage energy > `desired_storage_amount(Energy)`), read from **ADR 0014's posture/arbitration** once it lands (war posture suppresses processing; peacetime surplus runs it flat-out). The energy *deposit generator* gets the same gate so haulers stop feeding a throttled spawn, and its **priority map is un-inverted** (emptier → higher priority, matching every other refill in the codebase).
2. **`OPERATE_POWER` integration (later, with §D3):** a level-10+ operator effect takes the room from 1 → up to 6 power/t (300 e/t drain) — the gate above is what makes that safe to leave wired.

**GPL strategy:** process **everything** acquired (power has no other consumer until ops/market maturity; selling power is a 0012 fair-value decision, default *don't* — the flywheel values it above market). Milestones, from `1000·n²`: GPL 2 = 4k power (≈ 1–2 banks) → first operator at L1; GPL 8 = 64k → L7 (OPERATE_SPAWN r3, ×0.5); GPL 15 = 225k → L14 (r4); **GPL 23 = 529k power = 26.5M processing energy → L22 (OPERATE_SPAWN r5)**. At the 2-bank concurrency ceiling (~1.5–2 power/t income) that is a 300k-tick program — start the accrual at Increment 1, not Increment 8. MMO accelerant: **buy power** under 0012's price guards (dominant bots do; the harness can't see it — G-15 caveat applies).

### D3 — Operator design (the Increment 8 anchor)

A single **`OperatorSystem`** (ECS resource + system, mirroring the Squad Manager's reconciler shape) owns: creation/upgrade intents, per-room assignment, the per-tick action scheduler, renew/retreat policy, and the ops ledger. All of its state is **derivable** — builds are readable from the account creep's `powers` map, assignment is a pure function of (GPL allowance, owned-room scores) — so it adds **no serialized state** (ADR 0002 discipline; a VM reset re-derives everything from the game API).

**D3.1 Build order (the decision, with justification).** First creep, **"Hub Operator"**, level-by-level:

| Creep level | Take | Why this order |
|---|---|---|
| L1 | GENERATE_OPS r1 | **The faucet comes first**: every economic power except REGEN_* costs ops, and GENERATE_OPS is the only in-game source (`usePower.js:57-69`). An ops-less operator can only haul. |
| L2–L3 | OPERATE_SPAWN r1→r2 | [0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) proves spawn-time is the binding constraint; r2 (×0.7) is +43% room throughput, the steepest marginal gain available this early (gate array [0,2,…]). |
| L4 | OPERATE_EXTENSION r1 | 2 ops per 20%-capacity instant refill — the cheapest ops→spawn-uptime conversion in the table; directly serves 0011 D2's refill-stall signal. |
| L5–L6 | GENERATE_OPS r2, OPERATE_TOWER r1 | fund the habit; first defensive power on the shelf. |
| L7–L9 | OPERATE_SPAWN r3 (×0.5), GENERATE_OPS r3, OPERATE_LAB r1 | r3 doubles throughput; 0.08 ops/t generation. |
| L10–L13 | REGEN_SOURCE r1→r3 + OPERATE_POWER r1 | the L10 gate opens the **free** (+e/t) powers and the GPL accelerator. |
| L14–L21 | OPERATE_SPAWN r4, GENERATE_OPS r4, OPERATE_LAB r2-r3, OPERATE_TOWER r2, REGEN_SOURCE r4 | |
| L22–L25 | **OPERATE_SPAWN r5 (×0.2)**, GENERATE_OPS r5, then OPERATE_CONTROLLER r1 / OPERATE_FACTORY r1 per posture | the L20/L22 gates; the 5× milestone. |

Alternative orderings argued and rejected: *OPERATE_SPAWN-only rush* (skipping GENERATE_OPS ranks) starves itself — see the ops budget below; *REGEN_SOURCE-first* is blocked by its L10 gate, moot; *OPERATE_CONTROLLER-first* is gated at L20, moot. Second creep (**"Field/Economy Operator"**, created once GPL allowance permits without delaying the first creep's L22): GENERATE_OPS, REGEN_SOURCE→r5, OPERATE_POWER, REGEN_MINERAL — the free-income specialist that also escorts bank duos (renews at banks, D1.4). Re-speccing via delete costs 24 real-time hours (`deletePowerCreep.js:27`) — builds are forever-ish; this table is the recorded decision.

**D3.2 The ops economy (honest budget).** Generation is the hard wall: one operator generates ≤ **0.16 ops/t** (r5: 8 per 50t cooldown). Sustained costs: OPERATE_SPAWN 0.1 ops/t **per spawn** (100 ops/1000t), OPERATE_LAB 0.01/t per lab, OPERATE_CONTROLLER & OPERATE_POWER 0.2/t each, OPERATE_TOWER 0.1/t per tower (wartime), DISRUPT/FORTIFY 1–2 ops/t (siege bursts). Full-suite for one RCL8 room ≈ **0.8 ops/t — five times one operator's generation**. Consequences, in order: (1) below GENERATE_OPS r4, **OPERATE_SPAWN is cast selectively** — timed to 0011 group-admission windows (big quads, war surges) and defense spikes, not sustained; (2) ops are a **stockpiled resource** like 0010's compounds — the operator banks its store (100+100×level; 2,300 at L22) into storage on a cadence, target floor **5,000 ops per operator room** before sustained effects switch on; (3) on MMO, **buy ops** through 0012 (they trade cheap relative to their spawn-throughput value); (4) ops/power hauling is ordinary [0007](0007-hauling-logistics.md) traffic (`RESOURCE_OPS` is a normal store resource; power creeps issue `withdraw`/`transfer` like creeps).

**D3.3 Per-room schedule (derived, the "can one operator run N spawns" answer).** Cooldowns are **per-power per-creep** (`usePower.js:25,291-296`), one `usePower` per tick:

- **OPERATE_SPAWN: cd 300 / duration 1000 → one operator sustains exactly ⌊1000/300⌋ = 3 concurrent spawn effects** — an RCL8 room's full 3 spawns with 100 ticks of slack, casting at t, t+300, t+600, refresh at t+900. **One operator cannot cover a second room's spawns**; spawn-pumping is one-operator-per-room by engine arithmetic.
- OPERATE_LAB: cd 50 / dur 1000 → up to 20 effects; the 10-lab cluster costs 10 casts + 100 ops per kilotick.
- REGEN_SOURCE: cd 100 / dur 300 → 3 effects sustained; 2 sources covered with wander slack.
- OPERATE_CONTROLLER / OPERATE_POWER / OPERATE_FACTORY / OPERATE_TERMINAL / OPERATE_STORAGE: cd 800/500 vs dur 1000 → ~1 effect each; all fit in the same creep's spare ticks (total ≤ ~40 casts/kilotick vs 1,000 available).
- **Cast scheduling is a pure kernel**: `(powers, cooldowns, room effect end-times, ops ledger, posture) → next cast` — an EDF queue over effect expiries, host-testable ([0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md)), governor-aware (defense casts never shed; economy casts shed at Conserve — consistent with [0004](0004-cpu-governance-and-load-shedding.md)).
- **Geometry:** all OPERATE powers are range 3 — [0009](0009-room-planning-and-multiroom-layout.md) records an **operator anchor tile** in the plan (Chebyshev ≤3 to all three spawns + storage; the hub stamp already clusters these), like 0010's boost tile.

**D3.4 Assignment & lifecycle.** Assignment: rank owned RCL8 rooms by spawn-pressure × war exposure (seg-57 spawn-uptime KPI, 0011); the Hub Operator parks in the top room; reassignment is rare and travel is cheap (1 tile/tick, no fatigue). Lifecycle invariants: **renew before `ageTime − travel_to_power_spawn − 200`** (the power spawn is in-base — renew is a ~20-tick errand per 5,000); **never die** — death costs 8 *real-time* hours of all effects (`_diePowerCreep.js:57`), so the retreat threshold is paranoid (leave at <50% hits or any hostile player creep in range 5; under siege, sit under a rampart — operators are valid rampart campers) and the threat response treats "operator at risk" as a Defense-class event. Death is *not* level loss — recovery is waiting, not regrinding.

### D4 — enableRoom policy & the attackability trade-off (engine-verified)

What `isPowerEnabled` actually exposes (this corrects folklore in both directions):

1. Enabling is **symmetric**: once true, ANY user's operator can use powers in the room — including DISRUPT_SPAWN/TOWER/SOURCE/TERMINAL against you (`usePower.js:9-12` checks only the flag, not ownership).
2. Refusing to enable is **not a shield**: an enemy operator at range 1 to your controller enables it for you, blocked only by active safe mode (`enableRoom.js:12-19`), and the flag never reverts while you hold the room (`controllers\tick.js:52-62`).
3. Safe mode is the **defender's trump**: with safe mode active, non-owner operators can use **no powers at all** (`usePower.js:13-15`) and cannot enableRoom — but the owner's operators are unaffected. Power asymmetry under safe mode favors the defender absolutely.

**Policy:** enable every owned room **when its first operator deploys** (not before — an enabled room with no operator is pure downside), in one recorded action per room. Rationale: the marginal attacker-enablement we grant is small (a serious attacker brings a throwaway L0 operator and enables anyway — creation costs 1 GPL and nothing else), while *not* enabling denies us OPERATE_TOWER/FORTIFY/SHIELD in exactly the siege where they decide the room. Mitigations that make the trade safe: controller approach hardening ([0009](0009-room-planning-and-multiroom-layout.md): wall/rampart the controller's range-1 ring — upgraders work from range 3), the D5.3 counter-operator doctrine, and safe mode as the hard stop.

### D5 — Doctrine

**D5.1 Defensive powers (extends 0008 `Defend` / G-10).** Pre-staged, never brewed reactively (tick-snapshot discipline): the ops floor (D3.2) is Tier-A-style insurance. Under `ThreatLevel ≥ PlayerRaid`: OPERATE_TOWER on the focus towers (×1.5 → 5,400 > the 4,800 quad-heal line — the deterrence number); **FORTIFY rotation** on the breach rampart (5 ops per ≤5t of *non-nuke invulnerability*, `_damage.js:26-31` — at r5, one rampart held invulnerable indefinitely for ~1 ops/t; rMA can't even target it, `rangedMassAttack.js:47-49`); SHIELD as emergency cover (instant 25k rampart on the operator's tile); OPERATE_EXTENSION to keep defense waves spawning at zero refill latency. Defense casts ride the never-shed tier and, like 0010's boosts, **never gate** defense — they upgrade it.

**D5.2 Offensive DISRUPT (extends 0008 assault objectives — post-Inc-8 maturity).** An operator slot in the `ForceRequirement`: DISRUPT_TOWER's cd 0 / dur 5 / range 50 lets one operator hold **5 towers at ×0.5 simultaneously** (rotate one cast/tick; 10 ops/t) — 6 RCL8 towers drop from 3,600 to ~2,100 dmg/t at optimal, trivially inside a boosted quad's pooled heal; DISRUPT_SPAWN (range 20) freezes the defense spawn during the breach; DISRUPT_TERMINAL (L20+) cuts resupply. Hard limits the plan must encode: all of it is dead while the target's **safe mode** is active (time assaults to expiry, or to a nuke landing — nukes cancel safe mode), and the target room must be power-enabled (it is, if they run operators; if not, our operator enables it — `enableRoom` works on hostile controllers). Ops budget ~10–12 ops/t of siege — war-chest math, on the 0014 ledger.

**D5.3 Defending against enemy operators (retrofit to 0008's threat model + threatmap).** A hostile power creep in an owned/reserved room is a **first-class threat type** (today `threatmap.rs` only sees creep DPS/heal): classification `HostileOperator{level≈hits/1000, in_range_of(spawns|towers|controller)}`. Response doctrine, in order: (1) **focus-fire it** — 23k hits at L22 with no self-heal power and creep-style damage netting (`power-creeps\tick.js:49-67`) dies to 6 focused towers (3,600/t → ~7 ticks) anywhere in falloff range; it must hide behind ramparts or allied heals to persist; (2) **deny the controller ring** (D4 hardening) so a non-enabled room stays non-enabled; (3) **safe mode** if DISRUPT pressure threatens a breach — it switches off every enemy power instantly (`usePower.js:13-15`); (4) intel: sighting an operator feeds G-1 player profiling (a power-running opponent is a different threat class — their rooms also carry the OPERATE_TOWER deterrence number, which the 0008 force planner must price in: assume ×1.5 towers in any power-enabled enemy room).

### D6 — Retrofit notes to sibling ADRs (explicit, so none re-decides)

- **[0011](0011-spawn-orchestration.md) (spawn orchestration):** OPERATE_SPAWN/OPERATE_EXTENSION become **inputs, not constants**. OPERATE_EXTENSION changes the **refill-trend stall detector** semantics (instant refills produce step functions, not trends). The orchestrator additionally becomes a *requester*: when group admission is blocked on lane time, it may request an OPERATE_SPAWN cast (priority-ranked against the ops ledger).
- **[0010](0010-boost-lab-factory-pipeline.md) (labs/factory):** the **`OPERATE_FACTORY` invariant resolves here** — *keep level-0-only until an operator with the target rank exists AND the 0012 chain-vs-inputs price check passes; then brand deliberately*: one factory per chain tier, recorded (room, level, tick) in the ops log, chosen in a terminal-connected room ([0009] adjacency). Until that recorded decision, the 0010 debug-assert stands. `OPERATE_LAB` re-rates 0010's planner arithmetic (÷3 at r5) — the ReagentPlanner's batch ETA gains an `effective_output(lab)` input. REGEN_MINERAL (free ops) extends mineral income on the same schedule kernel.
- **[0006](0006-eval-and-iteration-harness.md) (harness): GPL/state seeding is mandatory.** You cannot replay 529k processed power per scenario. Power state is backend-side: `user.power` (GPL progress) and the `users.power_creeps` collection (account creeps) live in the launcher's mongo, and `controller.isPowerEnabled`/power-spawn stores are room objects. The harness bootstrap gains a **`PowerFixture`** step — direct mongo writes (the bollard/CLI path 0006 already owns) setting `user.power`, inserting pre-built operator documents (level/powers per D3.1 milestones), placing power in storage, and flagging `isPowerEnabled` — so Inc-8 scenarios start *at* GPL 8/15/23 instead of grinding to them. - **[0008](0008-combat-and-squad-architecture.md) (combat):** `Farm{powerbank}` objective carries D1.2's composition + the kill-deadline; threat model gains `HostileOperator` (D5.3) and prices enemy OPERATE_TOWER into force plans; assault objectives gain the optional operator slot (D5.2). - **[0007](0007-hauling-logistics.md)/[0012](0012-market-and-risk.md):** power/ops are ordinary resources on existing seams; 0012 adds two list entries — ops/power on the **buy** programs (posture-gated) and power on the **never-sell-while-flywheel-hungry** list; ops join the wartime embargo list (selling ops to a besieger arms his operator).

The D2 surplus gate is the interim stand-in until 0014 lands.

## Alternatives Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Two-half pillar: accrual fixes ride Inc 1–7, OperatorSystem anchors Inc 8 (chosen)** | GPL compounds for ~6 increments before a line of operator code exists; every piece lands on an existing seam; the expensive half waits for the substrate (M7) it needs | Inc 8 is a big bite (new system + doctrine); ops economy adds a tunable surface |
| **Ignore power entirely (status quo trajectory)** | Zero work | Concedes a 5× spawn / 3× lab / +33 e/t / 130 GCL/t multiplier class to every power-running opponent; the gap analysis's "difference in kind between tiers" — unacceptable for the dominance goal |
| **Process-and-bank GPL only, never spend (G-2b alone)** | Cheap; GPL is never lost | GPL with no operator is a scoreboard; 26.5M energy of processing with zero return — strictly worse than not processing |
| **Operators first, banks later (buy power on market)** | Skips combat dependency | Inverts the dependency for MMO-credit reasons the harness can't validate; bank farming is also the cheap half (code exists, two fixes) — buying *supplements* (D2), it shouldn't replace |
| **Operators as Squad-Manager-managed squad members** | One lifecycle owner | Wrong lifetime model (5,000t + renew-resets + 8h real-time death cooldown vs. 1,500t pre-spawn doctrine); no spawn lane contention; forcing them through 0008's reconciler distorts both — a dedicated OperatorSystem with an `escort` directive is smaller |
| **Never enable owned rooms (deny DISRUPT)** | Feels safe | Engine-refuted: the enemy enables your room themselves (`enableRoom.js:12-19`, safe-mode-gated only); you'd forfeit OPERATE_TOWER/FORTIFY/SHIELD in sieges for a lock the attacker holds the key to |
| **Enable all rooms immediately (before operators)** | One less state | Grants symmetric attacker access (D4.1) while we field zero powers — all downside until an operator parks there |
| **Higher-class power creeps later (multi-class design)** | Future-proof | The engine ships exactly one class, `operator` (`POWER_CLASS`, `constants.js:815-817`); designing for phantom classes is speculation |

## Consequences

**Positive**
- The five engine multipliers (spawn ×5, lab ×3, towers ×1.5, +33 e/t/room, 130 GCL/t) become reachable, each wired into the sibling ADR that already owns its consumer — closing the gap map's anchor item with no re-decisions.
- The accrual half is nearly free: two `war.rs` fixes + a throttle ride increments that exist, so by Inc 8 the GPL is *already banked* (the 300k-tick lesson of D2 is paid in calendar time, not rework).
- Bank-farming arithmetic is corrected before it wastes squads (the 25A/25H healer deficit and the `+500` decay fudge are both pre-deployment catches — cheaper than the post-mortem).
- Defense doctrine gains its strongest primitives (FORTIFY invulnerability, OPERATE_TOWER over the quad line) exactly where G-10 said deterrence binds.
- Everything decision-bearing is a pure kernel (go/no-go window, build order table, cast scheduler, ops ledger) — host-testable per 0006, harness-provable via the PowerFixture.

**Negative / new risks**
- **Real-time coupling enters the design** (8h death cooldown, 24h delete — `Date.now()`, not ticks): the retreat policy is doctrine-critical, and the harness can compress ticks but not wall-clock — death-avoidance gets a kernel test, not a faithful scenario.
- **Ops under-generation** (D3.2's 0.8-vs-0.16 gap) means sustained full-suite operation is a *late*-game state; mis-scheduling ops (e.g. sustaining OPERATE_SPAWN at r1 while defense floor is empty) is the new mis-tiering risk — same mitigation as 0010: priority ordering asserted by a harness invariant.
- **Enabled rooms are attackable via powers** (D4) — accepted, mitigated, and priced into the threat model rather than wished away.
- One more empire system (OperatorSystem) with tunables (ops floor, retreat thresholds, assignment weights). Bounded: no serialized state, pure kernels, config-driven.
- The pillar's value concentrates in 1–2 unkillable-by-policy units; an operator trapped or repeatedly zoned out of its room degrades multiple multipliers at once. Mitigation: the anchor tile is inside the rampart shell ([0009]), and uptime telemetry makes degradation visible.

**CPU & tick-safety.** Cast load is ≤ ~40 intents/kilotick/operator (~0.008 CPU/t) plus one cheap scheduler pass; bank squads/haulers are existing mission costs. Movement uses the 0004 facade. No new panic surface: all game-object resolution is the standard handled-`None` discipline; the OperatorSystem runs inside 0005's tick containment; under Conserve/Critical it sheds economy casts and re-planning, never defense casts (0004 shed order).
