# Ibex Incremental Rewrite Plan — 2026-06-09

> Built from [`../reviews/ibex-review-report.md`](../reviews/) and the [`../design/`](../design/) ADRs. **Incremental & confidence-driven**; back-compat not required (serialized state may be dropped per step). Don't break the running bot mid-increment.

## 1. North-Star & Objective Function
- **Win conditions:** survive · expand · score. Ibex is a competently-designed, feature-complete economic bot, but it is not yet top-tier: it cannot reliably convert an economic lead into territory, expansion is route-blind, and — decisively — **it can permanently die to its own CPU use** (review §Exec, Field Report C). The rewrite optimizes for converting "viable on a single quiet colony" into "viable under sustained multi-colony war."
- **"Colony health" score** (review §9 — *the* objective function the rewrite optimizes and self-improvement diffs against). Four weighted terms, **survival-dominating**, fixed-weight & per-scenario-normalized so the score is reproducible and regression-diffable:
  1. **Survival** (gate + score) — avoided extinction: no death-spiral, no unrecoverable deser failure, spawn alive. A spiral run scores ~0 here **regardless of every other term**.
  2. **CPU headroom** — mean/p95 of `(tick_limit − cpu_used)/tick_limit` **including intent cost**, plus long-tick rate.
  3. **Economic growth** — slope of GCL + stored energy + RCL-progress.
  4. **Military win-rate** — rooms held vs lost, **squad cohesion rate** (fraction of combat ticks with all members in-range — directly measures Field Report A), targets killed vs waves spent.
- **Non-negotiables (hard invariants every increment must preserve):**
  - **Never die to our own CPU** — bucket-aware load-shedding must keep the colony making progress under pressure; the MIN_PATHFIND_OPS floor stays so creeps never fully freeze (IBEX-003/016).
  - **Never silently lose state** — deserialization failure and segment overflow must become *loud, deterministic* events (reject-and-reset, watermark), never a spontaneous empty world (IBEX-004/014); the only state drops allowed are the **two labelled, intentional, low-stakes-tick resets** in Increments 2 and 5.
  - **Never break the running bot mid-increment** — strangler-fig: one stable seam per step, harness-verified before the next.

## 4. Milestones & Sequencing
- **M0 — Verifiable (end of Inc 0):** harness can deploy + advance ticks + capture the metrics segment; colony-health score computable; baseline recorded under `runs/` keyed by (scenario, git SHA).
- **M1 — Survivable (end of Inc 1):** under induced CPU pressure + forced reset the bot makes progress and never restart-loops; no tick ever skips `serialize_world`; the extinction-class risk is closed. *This is the table-stakes milestone — the bot is competitively viable only after it.*
- **M2 — Durable (end of Inc 2):** schema drift and overflow are loud, deterministic events; no spontaneous empty-world resets. (First intentional low-stakes reset lands here.)
- **M3 — Identifiable (end of Inc 3):** every squad ref resolves the same logical squad across reset/recycle; dangling-ref counter at zero.
- **M4 — Coordinated (end of Inc 4):** defense and offense squads hold formation (cohesion rate above threshold); stuck/finished campaigns tear down on a deadline. Field Reports A/B closed.
- **M5 — Clean (end of Inc 5):** tagged format + id-keyed ownership; `repair_entity_integrity` deleted. (Second intentional low-stakes reset lands here.)
- **M6 — Evolvable (end of Inc 6):** data-driven FSM proven at parity on HaulJob and rolling out; transient errors tolerated. Field Report F closed.
- **M7 — Expansive (end of Inc 7):** route-aware expansion + planned base↔remote roads (multi-room layout); haulers sized by route distance; boost pipeline + market hardening landed; (optionally) incubation. The bot can convert an economic lead into territory — the competitive verdict's stated weakness closed.
- **M8 — Compounding (end of Inc 8):** operators running (`OPERATE_SPAWN` multiplier empirically proven via spawn-utilization %); boosted upgraders doubling GCL at the cap; player-level intel driving war/peace through the 0014 posture engine; nuke doctrine + Recovery posture live. The dominance flywheel (squads → power → throughput → economy → squads) is spinning.
- **M9 — Dominant (Inc 9 menu, evidence-gated):** temple GCL cycling, deposits/commodities, the automated campaign planner — entered only as seg-57 evidence shows the preceding engines saturated.

## 5. Per-Increment Validation Strategy
The full testing program now lives in **[ADR 0015](../design/0015-testing-and-validation-strategy.md)** (taxonomy L0–L6, seam-contract registry, fast-iteration policy) and **[component-test-plans.md](component-test-plans.md)** (per-component plans + the consolidated testing-roadmap table — fold that table into §3 at roadmap time). Increment gates run in **"increment gate mode"** (N=9 on-demand seeded runs that may gate immediately); the earned-gating soak rule applies only to the nightly lane. The three checks below remain the spine; 0015 absorbs and extends them.

Every increment is gated by the **same three harness checks** before the next begins (review §9 pre-deploy gates + §8 sequencing):
- **Harness smoke-run** — deploy → run K ticks on the economy-bringup scenario with **zero deser failures, zero caught panics, zero segment-overflow drops, CPU under `tick_limit`, no death-spiral alarm**; colony-health score not regressed below the (scenario, git SHA) baseline beyond threshold.
- **Serialization round-trip** — encode→decode identity on the touched components, plus the old-snapshot corpus and fuzz (Inc 2+ exercise this hardest; the encode/decode helpers are pure and a `MemoryArbiter` double makes the pipeline testable today).
- **Replay parity** — record real `GameView` reads, replay old-vs-new behind a flag, diff the emitted intents (parity is the explicit gate for the Inc 1 scheduler seam and the Inc 6 FSM pilot before cutover).

**Per-increment focus:** Inc 0 stands the substrate up (smoke-run is the deliverable). Inc 1 adds CPU-pressure + forced-reset replay. Inc 2/Inc 5 add the round-trip/old-snapshot/fuzz suite as the hard gate. Inc 3 adds the squad-identity round-trip + index-recycle test. Inc 4 adds the cohesion-metric scenario assertion. Inc 6 adds HaulJob intent-diff parity.

**Intentional one-time resets** (Inc 2 and Inc 5) are confined to **low-stakes ticks** (a quiet economic window, no active war, GCL/RCL not mid-milestone), labelled in advance, and announced as Memory/format breaks. They are the *only* sanctioned state drops; every other increment is None- or Behavioral-breaking and preserves serialized state.

## 6. Risks & Rollback
Lead with the two survival-class risks; each step is independently reversible because it sits behind a stable seam (revert the seam, keep the running bot).
